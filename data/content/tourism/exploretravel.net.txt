Explore Romania means:       • Border land of Europe, a country where East and West mix in a natural and creative way • Effervescent society where changes happen in a dramatic rhythm, as the 3rd millennium western civilization is taking over a way of life that hadn’t changed much since the 19th century • Marked by fifty years of communist dictatorship and more than twenty years of transition to the free market economy • Kind people and spectacular countryside • The Carpathians Mountains – some of the largest remaining areas of pristine forest on the continent, hosting the largest population of large carnivores in Europe • The Danube Delta – UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and Europe’s largest wetland • The fertile plains – quality soil for agriculture • UNESCO sites: painted monasteries in Bucovina, fortified churches in Transylvania, wooden churches of Maramures, monastery of Horezu, Dacian fortresses, the citadel of Sighisoara • Transylvania, the land that inspired Bram Stoker’s famous Dracula novel. • Interaction with the people and nature that we encounter, leaving as little impact as possible on the environment and using the facilities and the services provided by the locals, thus trying to preserve and encourage the communities • Exploration – “off the beaten track” in all our activities • Always remaining in contact with our traditional values, with nature and with the peasants’ common sense.        These would be some of our main reasons to invite you here, and we are hoping that you will discover on our website something to call you to this part of

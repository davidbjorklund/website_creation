Looking for YOUR PLACE IN ACADIA ? You will find it here! At Acadia Cottage Rentals we offer every kind of vacation rental, from small waterfront or rural cabins to cottages of all sizes in a variety of village, rural and waterfront settings and family-gathering compounds on the oceanfront. Bar Harbor and the Mt. Desert Island area offer an unlimited variety of vacation rental lodging options in an amazingly accessible natural environment. Acadia National Park has mountains to climb, forests to hike, world-class biking roads, and of course many miles of classic rocky Maine beaches for you to explore and relish. Here “back to nature” really means something — the word we hear most often is “awesome!”. You can quickly and easily get away from it all in the silence and beauty of unspoiled, uncrowded nature and still be within a few minutes of the comforts, services and entertainments of village life. Our unique and diverse collection of both bustling and quiet villages, the natural wildness and rough beauty of Acadia National Park, along with our gentle island ambiance make this the famous “Vacation Land” commemorated on our state vehicle tags. Find serenity and excitement here, but mostly find restoration and renewal. Many find tears in their eyes when they reluctantly leave this unique island, and everyone vows to return as soon as they are able — it’s that kind of place. Prime time weeks are booking quickly, and spring and fall seasons have been discovered as favorite times for many vacationers, so check out our listings and inquire about your favorites today. Availability changes every day as more travelers are drawn to this very special place in the world. Once you’ve explored the listings using the SEARCH options or the Cottage Locations Map  on this page, or sorting our cottage listings according to your needs and preferences on the Search Our Listings page, the best way to inquire or book your selection is to click the View Calendar/Quote button on the cottage listing pages & complete the Booking Request Form .
